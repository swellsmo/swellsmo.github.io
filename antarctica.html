Tensile Stresses of Antarctic Ice Shelves

Investigating the tensile strength of ice on Antarctic Ice Shelves using observations of strain and estimates of stress

Determining the stresses at which ice breaks is important in predicting future calving events and how they will affect the flow of ice upstream. To do this, I identify active crevasses on Landsat-8 derived strain rate fields and MODIS Mosaic of Antarctica optical imagery. I then sample principal stresses along each crevasse, and compare that to the principal stresses of unfractured ice. Plotting this data, I am able to draw yield criteria around the data, tuned visually by modifying the tensile strength used to plot each criterion.

 

My results observationally support a tensile strength of ~100kPa, which is commonly used by the modeling community. Additionally, I find that the Drucker-Prager yield criterion fits the data better than the von Mises criterion. I currently have a manuscript on this work in preparation for publication.
